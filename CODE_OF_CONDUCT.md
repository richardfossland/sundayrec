# Code of Conduct

SundayRec is built primarily for churches, parishes and faith communities, and the people who contribute to it come from many backgrounds, denominations and points of view. We want the project's spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions and email — to feel welcoming and useful to everyone involved.

This Code of Conduct describes what we expect from each other.

## In short

**Be kind. Be patient. Help people.**

Most of the people opening issues here are volunteers spending their Sunday afternoon trying to get a USB mixer to work. Treat them as you would want a parishioner to be treated.

## What we expect

Contributors and participants — including the maintainer — agree to:

- **Be respectful.** Disagree with ideas, not with people. Critique the code, not the coder.
- **Be patient with non-technical users.** Many SundayRec users are not developers. "RTFM" is never an acceptable response.
- **Assume good intent.** When something seems wrong, ask first; accuse later (or not at all).
- **Use plain language.** This applies to comments, commit messages and especially documentation. SundayRec serves a non-technical audience; clarity matters more than cleverness.
- **Respect differing viewpoints.** Theological, denominational and political differences are inevitable in a project used by many kinds of churches. Keep discussions focused on the software.
- **Welcome newcomers.** Help first-time contributors find their way around the codebase.

## What we don't accept

- Personal attacks, insults, or any form of harassment
- Discriminatory remarks based on race, ethnicity, nationality, gender, sexual orientation, age, disability, religion or denomination
- Sexualised language or imagery
- Trolling, sea-lioning, or deliberately inflammatory comments
- Publishing someone else's private information (e.g. emails, IP addresses, location) without permission
- Using the project's communication channels to advertise unrelated products, services or causes

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct, please contact the maintainer at **hello@sundayrec.com**. All reports will be reviewed and handled with discretion.

The maintainer reserves the right to:

- Remove comments, commits, code or issues that don't align with this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor who behaves in a way they consider harmful to the community
- Decline contributions for any reason

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — the GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, and any direct communication regarding SundayRec.
